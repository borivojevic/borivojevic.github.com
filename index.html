Hiring A Developer Doesn't Need To Feel Like Pulling Teeth

Have your company already hired freelancers in the past? Did they understand you and your business? Did your project end up on time and on budget? I know exactly how you feel - not long ago I was clumsy freelancer too. However, over time I adopted few key aspects that helped me understand my clients better, deliver my services on time and make my clients happier.

1. Programming is about communication as much it is about writing a great code. Good developers must know to recognize actual problems and to communicate requirements and business goals effectively before turning them into language of machines. My development process focuses on frequent and clear communication with clients in order to understand problems they have and help them define goals before we start figuring out the perfect solution.

2. Writing clean, readable and testable code is one of my top priorities. Software developers spend only fraction of time writing code and majority of their time reading and maintaining it. Having great, maintainable code can save lot of time, effort and client's money in the long run.

3. I take project management seriously and use agile software management techniques. We will start with working but incomplete prototype and continuously add features and refine it until you are fully satisfied. At any time I'll be able to answer you where are we on time and budget and what are we currently working on.

My Story

I got my first paid job in 2007 in local web development agency although I started learning programing almost 5 years earlier while I was still in high-school.

For next 5 years I was working on mid-to-large scale web applications, usually as a one-man-team and I learned how to cope with big projects alone. That gave me flexibility and challenge to develop skills in other technical areas such as setting up production servers, deploying, monitoring and maintaining applications as well as non-technical areas like communicating requirements directly with clients, writing project proposals, writting technical specifications and estimates and managing software products development.

Working and being managed remotely throughout almost whole my career gave me skills to communicate and collaborate effectively with remote teams.
